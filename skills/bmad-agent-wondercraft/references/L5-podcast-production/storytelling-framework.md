# L5 Podcast Storytelling Framework

## Purpose

This reference gives Wanda a production-level structure for educational podcast
episodes. The goal is not decorative narrative; it is retention, clarity, and a
reason for the learner to keep listening.

## Core Arc

1. Hook: open with a concrete tension, misconception, or decision.
2. Context: locate the issue inside the learner's current task.
3. Conflict: make the cost of the wrong mental model explicit.
4. Development: introduce the concept through examples and contrast.
5. Resolution: show the corrected model working on the original tension.
6. Call to action: ask for one next learner behavior.

## Educational Hook Patterns

1. Mistake-first: "Most teams break this when..."
2. Decision-first: "You have two paths; only one preserves..."
3. Artifact-first: "Look at this transcript, log, or output..."
4. Constraint-first: "The budget, time, or policy forces..."
5. Role-first: "The reviewer, operator, or learner needs..."

## Episode Shape Guidance

Use the smallest story frame that carries the lesson. A three-minute explainer
can use one tension and one resolution. An eight-minute episode can support a
turning point. A fifteen-minute story arc needs recurring stakes, not more
facts pasted together.

## Dialogue Guidance

Dialogue should create cognitive contrast. The learner voice may ask the
question a real operator would ask. The expert voice should answer with
specific evidence, then give the learner a way to test the answer.

## Anti-Patterns

1. Do not open with generic welcome copy.
2. Do not let backstory delay the first useful idea.
3. Do not add characters unless they carry a learning function.
4. Do not resolve the tension before the learner understands the error.
5. Do not close with broad encouragement; close with a concrete action.

## Production Checklist

1. State the hook in the first thirty seconds.
2. Name the learner problem before naming the framework.
3. Include one example with observable evidence.
4. Mark the transition into the resolution.
5. End with a task the learner can perform immediately.

## Revision Pass

1. Remove any sentence that only announces the episode exists.
2. Replace abstract stakes with a visible decision or artifact.
3. Check that each scene changes what the learner understands.
4. Keep the narrator focused on the learner's next move.
5. Preserve one through-line from hook to close.
6. Convert long explanations into contrast pairs.
7. Confirm every character voice has a learning job.
8. Confirm the final action can be completed without extra context.
9. Record unresolved operator questions in the handoff.
10. Do not promote draft language to production without a listen pass.
