# L5 Audio Production Patterns

## Purpose

This reference captures the audio-level choices Wanda should consider when
turning educational intent into a podcast artifact. It focuses on cadence,
pause discipline, music-bed behavior, and reviewable handoff cues.

## Voice Acting Cadence

1. Use a steady instructional pace for definitions.
2. Slow down before a key contrast or failure mode.
3. Let examples sound more conversational than definitions.
4. Avoid artificial enthusiasm in technical correction segments.
5. Use pitch movement to signal a transition, not to decorate every sentence.

## Pause Discipline

1. Add a short pause after a question that the listener should answer.
2. Add a medium pause after a misconception is named.
3. Add a short pause before the final action step.
4. Avoid long dead air in list-heavy explanations.
5. Keep pauses consistent across repeated segment structures.

## Music-Bed Volume Curve

1. Intro bed may start audible, then duck under narration quickly.
2. Default narration bed target is quiet enough to ignore.
3. Raise bed only during section transitions or outro.
4. Fade before technical definitions.
5. Never let the bed compete with names, numbers, or commands.

## Segment Construction

1. Introduce chapter markers at conceptual boundaries.
2. Keep each segment's first sentence self-contained.
3. Avoid burying a command in the middle of a musical transition.
4. Use one sonic cue per transition family.
5. Preserve silence around learner reflection prompts.

## Handoff Notes

Wanda should produce enough metadata for downstream assembly: title, target
duration, voice roles, chapter markers, style overrides, and any operator
listen-check required before publication.

## Quality Checklist

1. The first voice arrives before the listener has to infer context.
2. Music supports pacing but never carries meaning by itself.
3. Pauses make thinking room without implying a broken render.
4. Segment transitions are audible but restrained.
5. The outro repeats the action step without restating the whole episode.

## Review Pass

1. Listen for clipped starts at every segment boundary.
2. Listen for mouth noise or harsh consonants on emphasized terms.
3. Check whether the bed masks low-volume syllables.
4. Check whether pauses are long enough to think but short enough to trust.
5. Confirm the final export has predictable loudness.
6. Confirm the voice role remains stable across the episode.
7. Confirm no instruction is hidden under a transition.
8. Confirm chapter markers align with conceptual transitions.
9. Confirm operator notes name any manual assembly needs.
10. Confirm the artifact can be reviewed without opening Wondercraft.
