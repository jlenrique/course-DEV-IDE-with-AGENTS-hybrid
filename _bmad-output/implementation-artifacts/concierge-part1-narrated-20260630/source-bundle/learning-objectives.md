## Authoritative (fresher) — 5 numbered, Bloom-tagged objectives

Source-of-truth: `course-content/courses/tejal-APC-C1/C1M1Part01.md` lines 9-21
(byte-identical copy also present in `tejal-c1m1-fresh-outline/source-outline.md`
"C1M1: Gemini Updated" rendering, lines 326-331).

**By the end of this module, the learner will be able to:**

1. **Define** the "innovation mindset" and **correlate** core clinical competencies (e.g. diagnostic reasoning, empathy) directly to established innovation frameworks like Design Thinking. *(Understand / Apply)*
2. **Analyze** the macro-economic and structural trends—including administrative burnout, healthcare consumerism, and technological acceleration—that necessitate intrapreneurial physician leadership. *(Analyze)*
3. **Differentiate** between a superficial "idea" and a rigorously vetted "opportunity" using the core tenets of Sandra Lam's *Intrapreneurship Formula*. *(Analyze / Evaluate)*
4. **Evaluate** systemic operational failures within their own clinical environment by conducting root-cause analyses and defending their findings in a peer-reviewed forum (the "M&M of Innovation"). *(Evaluate)*
5. **Create** a personal innovation baseline (SWOT analysis) and an "Opportunity Radar" that captures and categorizes real-world clinical frictions for future project development. *(Create)*

**This module directly supports the following Course 1 Goal:**

> To equip learners with the foundational mindsets and skills to identify, validate, and articulate a compelling opportunity for innovation within their clinical environment.

**By the end of this module, the learner will be able to:**

- **MO 1.1:** Definition of the innovation mindset, why it is important and common misconceptions.
- **MO 1.2:** Analyze the impact of psychological safety and fixed/growth mindsets on clinical team performance, patient safety, and innovation.
- **MO 1.3:** Apply the "Intelligent Failure" framework to distinguish between blameworthy and praiseworthy failures in a clinical setting.
- **MO 1.4:** Evaluate one's personal innovation mindset, goals, and professional environment through a structured SWOT analysis.
- **MO 1.5:** Create an initial innovation portfolio by sourcing ideas from experience, future goals, and daily clinical observation.
