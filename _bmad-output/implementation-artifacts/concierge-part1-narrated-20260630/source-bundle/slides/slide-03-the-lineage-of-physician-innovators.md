## Examples of Innovative Leaders (verbatim)

### Emergency Physicians pioneering tele-triage
- Reference: [Emergency Physicians pioneering tele-triage](https://www.acep.org/siteassets/sites/acep/media/sections-documents/emi/acep-information-paper---rapid-application-of-technology-for-emergency-department-tele-triage-may-2020-1.pdf)
- **narration:** During the pandemic, emergency physicians rapidly implemented tele-triage systems to evaluate patients remotely, reduce exposure risk, and preserve hospital capacity. This was innovation in real time — clinicians redesigning care delivery under pressure using technology and adaptive problem-solving

### Clinical informatics examples: bring EMR to hospitals; tech; Jefferson adding Abridge
- **narration:** Clinicians are also driving innovation through informatics. The expansion of electronic medical records and tools like Abridge, which uses AI to summarize clinical conversations, shows how physician-led technology adoption can improve documentation efficiency, reduce burnout, and enhance patient communication.

### Glenn Steele
**Glenn Steele** led Geisinger Health System's transformation through systematic care redesign and innovation infrastructure. Under his leadership, Geisinger developed ProvenCare (a warranty-based surgical care model), ProvenHealth Navigator (an advanced medical home model with embedded nurse case managers), and established dedicated innovation teams supported by electronic health information systems
- Reference: [https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4106555/](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4106555/)
- **narration:** Under Glenn Steele's leadership, Geisinger Health System became a model for system-level innovation. Programs like ProvenCare bundled evidence-based protocols with outcome guarantees, while ProvenHealth Navigator embedded nurse care managers into primary care. These initiatives were supported by data infrastructure and dedicated innovation teams, demonstrating how leadership and systems design can transform care delivery

### Dr. Atul Gawande (Process Innovation via the Checklist)
**Dr. Atul Gawande (Process Innovation via the Checklist):** Recognizing that "brilliant problem-solvers" still make mistakes due to system complexity, he adapted the aviation industry's **Checklist Manifesto** for the OR. This wasn't a new drug or device; it was a **process innovation** that drastically reduced surgical mortality.
- Reference (attachment): WHO Surgical Safety Checklist PDF — `[UNRESOLVED-NOTION-FILE-ATTACHMENT: WHO_Surgical_Safety_Checklist.pdf]` (referenced in source; rendered file not available in this corpus)
- Reference: [The Checklist Manifesto: How to Get Things Right](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3960713/)
- **narration:** Gawande's work reinforces that clinical innovation often addresses process, not just technology. By recognizing that human error persists in complex environments, he reframed safety as a systems issue and introduced structured tools to improve reliability — a hallmark of innovation thinking
  - Atul Gawande recognized that even highly skilled surgeons operate within complex systems where preventable errors occur. By adapting a simple aviation-style checklist for surgery, he led a process innovation that significantly reduced complications and mortality worldwide — showing that innovation can be about systems and teamwork, not just technology

### Dr. Michelle Longmire (Digital Health Innovation - Medable)
**Dr. Michelle Longmire (Digital Health Innovation - Medable):** A dermatologist who saw how slow and inaccessible clinical trials were. She leveraged an innovation mindset to create a platform for **decentralized clinical trials**, making research more equitable and faster by removing the "brick-and-mortar" bottleneck.
- **narration:** As a dermatologist and researcher, Michelle Longmire saw firsthand how traditional clinical trials excluded many patients due to geographic and logistical barriers. She founded Medable to support decentralized trials, using digital tools to bring research into patients' homes — making studies faster, more inclusive, and more adaptable.
