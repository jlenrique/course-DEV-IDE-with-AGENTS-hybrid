## Narration (Speaker Notes)

"To navigate this lineage, you must cultivate two foundational elements: a **Growth Mindset** and **Psychological Safety**. A growth mindset, coined by Carol Dweck, is the belief that your capacity to innovate is not fixed; it is a muscle built through effort and critique. But a growth mindset cannot survive without psychological safety. Amy Edmondson defines this as a shared belief that the environment is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. As you progress through this certificate, you will be asked to share half-baked ideas, critique your peers, and expose systemic flaws. We must collectively commit to maintaining psychological safety in our discussion boards and peer reviews. If we cannot be vulnerable about what is broken, we cannot design what is next."

## Enrichment — formal definitions (verbatim, attributed source)

These fill the slide's "Having formal definitions" placeholders. They are SME content
for the *same* slide, captured from the fresh-outline so the definition boxes are not
empty. Clearly labeled as enrichment; operator may include or drop.

- **Dweck's Growth Mindset** (source-outline.md Page 7 Slide 1, line 183): "belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through effort, effective strategies, feedback, and learning from mistakes, rather than being fixed traits."
- **Edmondson's Psychological Safety** (source-outline.md Page 7 Slide 1, line 184): "shared belief within a team that it is safe to take interpersonal risks—such as speaking up, asking questions, admitting mistakes, or offering dissenting ideas—without fear of embarrassment, punishment, or retaliation."
- **First Principles Thinking** (source-outline.md "Gemini Updated" Slide 4.5 Innovation Lexicon, line 361): "Breaking down a complex, systemic problem into its most basic, foundational truths, and building a solution up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy or 'how we have always done it.'"
