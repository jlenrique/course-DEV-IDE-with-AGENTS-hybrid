# Slide 7: The "Wicked" World of Innovation (David Epstein)

**Visual Format:** Embedded YouTube Video ([Why the Most Experienced People Fail First](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3qiDR8nNpqc)) alongside a split-screen graphic: "Kind Environments (Clinical) vs. Wicked Environments (Innovation)."

> below), so it is retained as a slide asset rather than excluded as a standalone
> embedded-video component. Stable link preserved verbatim.

## Pre-Video Narration (Context)

"As clinical professionals, you have been rewarded for hyper-specialization. You have mastered 'Kind' learning environments, where patterns repeat and rules are clear. However, the systemic problems in healthcare are 'Wicked' environments. To solve them, you must embrace functional flexibility. Watch this breakdown by David Epstein on why generalists—those who can borrow tools from multiple domains—triumph in complex systems."

## Post-Video Summary Narration

"To be an effective healthcare intrapreneur, you cannot just be a hammer looking for a nail. You must have 'Range.' Functional flexibility means having the humility to drop the specific tools that made you a great clinician, and adopt the broad, interdisciplinary tools needed to redesign a broken system."
