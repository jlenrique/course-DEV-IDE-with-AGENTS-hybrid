# Slide 8: The Track vs. The Jungle (Functional Flexibility)

> Source labels this a **Design Brief** (visual-metaphor treatment), not "Slide N". Per
> operator instruction it is captured as its own numbered slide. It is the visual
> dramatization of the **Functional Flexibility / Kind-vs-Wicked** concept on Slide 7.

**The Core Message:** Clinical training prepares you for a predictable environment; system innovation requires a diverse toolkit for an unpredictable environment.

**Visual Layout:** A strict 50/50 split-screen graphic.

## The Composition

- **Left Side (The Track):** A sterile, brightly lit, red Olympic running track. It has clear white lines and perfectly spaced hurdles. A runner is in a starting block.
- **Right Side (The Jungle):** A dense, complex, pathless jungle environment. A figure stands at the edge, looking in, wearing a heavy backpack with a compass in hand, a machete strapped to their side, and a rope.

**Color Palette:** Left side: High contrast, sterile whites, and bright reds (representing the 'Kind' clinical environment). Right side: Deep, rich greens, earthy browns, and shadowed lighting (representing the 'Wicked' innovation environment).

## Required Text Labels

- Left Header: *"Kind Learning Environment (Clinical Specialization)"*
- Right Header: *"Wicked Learning Environment (Functional Flexibility)"*

## Speaker Notes (Narration)

*"To survive the hospital blueprint, you must develop 'Range.' As a clinician, you were trained on the track—a 'Kind' environment where the rules are clear and the hurdles are visible. But system innovation is the jungle. It is a 'Wicked' environment where the variables constantly change. You cannot bring track shoes to a jungle. You must be functionally flexible, willing to drop your specialized clinical tools and pick up a machete or a compass when the environment demands it."*
