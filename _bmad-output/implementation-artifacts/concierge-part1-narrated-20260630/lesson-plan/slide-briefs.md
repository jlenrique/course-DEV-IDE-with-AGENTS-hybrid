## Brief 5 — Slide c1m1-p1-s05
**Card title:** The Innovation Lexicon (Definition Box)

**On-card body (8-term reference box — verbatim terms):**
- **Innovation:** creating new value that is adopted by the market/system (not just inventing a new gadget).
- **Intrapreneurship:** acting like an entrepreneur but driving innovation from *within* an established organization (e.g. a hospital system) while navigating legacy constraints.
- **Systemic Friction:** chronic, recurring operational/clinical pain points that drain time, money, and morale.
- **Root Cause vs. Symptom:** the symptom is the visible pain (e.g. high nursing turnover); the root cause is the invisible system failure creating it (e.g. a broken EHR triage workflow).
- **Functional Flexibility:** the ability to adapt your skills and borrow concepts from outside your specialty to solve ambiguous problems.
- **First Principles Thinking:** breaking a complex systemic problem into its most basic foundational truths and building up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy or "how we've always done it." *(relocated here from Slide 4 per de-dupe §C)*
- **Psychological Safety:** the shared belief that a team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking — report errors, ask questions, propose unconventional ideas without fear of humiliation or retaliation. *(formal definition: Slide 4)*
- **Growth Mindset:** the belief that clinical and leadership capabilities are not fixed but built through dedication and iteration — shifting the failure response from "I am not equipped for this" to "What data did this failure give us?" *(formal definition: Slide 4)*

**Visual direction:** Clean, high-contrast **"Reference Box" slide** that learners can screenshot or download — 8 labeled definition rows, scannable, consistent typographic rhythm. Treat as a single reference artifact (keep as ONE card even if other slides cluster — lesson-plan Appendix F). Light cross-reference markers on Psychological Safety / Growth Mindset pointing to Slide 4's formal definitions.
