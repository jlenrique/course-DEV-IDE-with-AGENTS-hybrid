## C. DECISION ITEM 1 — Lexicon (Slide 5) <-> Slide 4 de-dupe recommendation

**Finding (from source, not re-derived):** 3 of Slide 5's 8 terms overlap Slide 4's staged definitions — *First Principles Thinking* (identical wording), *Psychological Safety* (different wording: Slide 4 = Edmondson formal; Lexicon = course-restated), *Growth Mindset* (different wording: Slide 4 = Dweck formal; Lexicon = course-restated, clinical-leadership-framed).

**Instructional reading of the two slides' DISTINCT functions:**
- **Slide 4 = the behavioral contract.** Two scholarly-attributed mindset definitions (Dweck, Edmondson) that the narration explicitly invokes to set the course's psychological-safety expectations. Register: formal, attributed, motivational.
- **Slide 5 = the operational reference box.** An 8-term, screenshot/download-able vocabulary spanning the whole problem-framing lexicon. Register: quick-reference, course-restated, utilitarian.

**RECOMMENDATION (for operator confirmation): KEEP BOTH, DIFFERENTIATE BY FRAMING + CROSS-REFERENCE, and RELOCATE First Principles.**
1. **First Principles Thinking (identical wording):** **Remove the First Principles definition box from Slide 4; keep it only in the Lexicon (Slide 5).** Rationale: Slide 4's narration names only *Growth Mindset* and *Psychological Safety*; First Principles is a lexicon term, not one of the two contracted frameworks. This also aligns Slide 4 with the newer "Gemini Updated" rendering, which already omits First Principles. Net: zero content lost (it lives on Slide 5), and Slide 4 stops carrying an orphan label its narration never speaks.
2. **Psychological Safety + Growth Mindset (different wording):** **Keep both versions** — they are not duplicates, they serve different functions. Slide 4 carries the **scholarly/attributed** definitions (the contract's grounding); Slide 5 carries the **course-restated operational** versions (the reference). Add a light cross-reference on the Slide 5 entries ("formal definition: Slide 4"). This is deliberate **spaced reinforcement** of the two keystone mindset terms across registers — pedagogically a feature, not redundancy.

**Net effect:** Slide 4 = exactly the two attributed frameworks the narration names; Slide 5 = the full 8-term reference with the two mindset terms cross-linked. No verbatim content dropped; the only change is relocating one identical First-Principles box. **Operator confirms before Gary renders.**
