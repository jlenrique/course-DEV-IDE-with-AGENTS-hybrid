- **Narration (Speaker Notes):** "Now that we know *how* you innovate, *where* do you do it? We often conflate innovation with Entrepreneurship—leaving the hospital to start a new company from scratch. But the most immediate impact you can make is through **Intrapreneurship**. An intrapreneur utilizes entrepreneurial principles to create, develop, and implement innovative solutions *within* an existing organization. It is navigating the constraints, politics, and resources of your current health system to improve it from the inside out. It requires a different type of political savvy, but it carries immense potential for scale. As Lam outlines, the formula requires balancing your clinical vision with your organizational environment, leveraging existing resources and mastering stakeholder alignment."

## Design Brief: The Intrapreneur's Maze (Blank Canvas vs. Blueprint)

- **The Core Message:** Intrapreneurship is harder than entrepreneurship because you must innovate without destroying the existing, load-bearing institutional structures.
- **Visual Layout:** A 50/50 split-screen graphic.
- **The Composition:**
	- **Left Side (The Entrepreneur):** A figure standing in a wide-open, empty green field under a blue sky, easily building a small house from scratch with a hammer.
	- **Right Side (The Intrapreneur):** A figure standing *inside* a highly complex, glowing neon-blue architectural blueprint of a massive hospital. They are trying to remodel a single room, but they are surrounded by thick, immovable "load-bearing" pillars.
- **Color Palette:** Left side: Bright, airy, and simple (green and light blue). Right side: Dark background with glowing "blueprint" lines (cyan, dark blue, and stark white).
- **Required Text Labels:**
	- Label on the left: *"Entrepreneur: Building on a blank canvas."*
	- Labels on the right pillars: *"Legacy IT," "Compliance," "Hospital Politics," "Operating Budgets."*
- **Speaker Notes (Narration):** *"Looking at our definitions, let’s visualize the difference between an entrepreneur and what you are doing—an intrapreneur. An entrepreneur gets to build a new company in an empty field. You do not have that luxury. You are building inside a highly complex, load-bearing blueprint. If you try to swing a sledgehammer without understanding the hospital's legacy IT systems, compliance rules, and operational budgets, you will bring the roof down. This course is about learning how to redesign the room without breaking the building."*
