## Design Brief: The Deconstruction (First Principles Thinking)

- **The Core Message:** Do not just iterate on "how we have always done it." Break the broken system down to its fundamental, undeniable truths, and build a new solution from scratch.
- **Visual Layout:** A 3-part horizontal progression (Left to Right).
- **The Composition:** 
	- **Phase 1 (Left):** A rigid, outdated, gray "Hospital Building" made entirely of interlocking toy blocks (like Legos). It looks clunky and patched together.
	- **Phase 2 (Middle):** The building is shown exploding/deconstructing. The complex building is broken all the way down into a neat, organized pile of basic, individual 1x1 raw blocks.
	- **Phase 3 (Right):** Those exact same raw blocks are now being reassembled into a sleek, modern, highly efficient, aerodynamic new structure.
- **Color Palette:** The "Old System" (Left) is dull gray and beige. The "Raw Blocks" (Middle) glow with primary colors. The "New System" (Right) is vibrant and illuminated.
- **Required Text Labels:** \* Under Phase 1: *"The Status Quo (Reasoning by Analogy)"*
	- Under Phase 2: *"First Principles (Breaking it down to fundamental truths)"*
	- Under Phase 3: *"The Innovation (Building a new system from the ground up)"*
- **Speaker Notes (Narration):** *"When you finally have a safe team and the functional flexibility to act, how do you actually rethink a broken workflow? You must use First Principles. Most healthcare leaders 'reason by analogy'—they look at the clunky, outdated hospital system they inherited and try to just add a fresh coat of paint to it. First Principles requires you to deconstruct the entire building. Break the workflow down into its absolute, raw, fundamental truths—the individual blocks. Only then can you take those same raw materials and build a fundamentally better, more efficient system from the ground up."*
