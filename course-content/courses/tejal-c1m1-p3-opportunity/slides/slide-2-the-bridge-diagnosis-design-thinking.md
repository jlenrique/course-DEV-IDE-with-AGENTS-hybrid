- **Prompt to Generate Image:** > *A side-by-side process flow diagram comparing two pathways. Pathway 1 (Left, Blue): 'The Clinical Method' showing steps: History → Physical/Labs → Diagnosis → Treatment Plan. Pathway 2 (Right, Green): 'Design Thinking' showing steps: Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype/Test. Draw visual connecting lines between History/Empathize, Diagnosis/Define, and Treatment/Prototype.*
- **Narration (Speaker Notes):** "Let's make this tangible. The most common framework for innovation is 'Design Thinking.' At first glance, it sounds like business jargon. But map it to your daily clinical workflow. When you take a patient's history, you are performing the first step of Design Thinking: **Empathize**. When you synthesize the physical exam and labs into a diagnosis, you are performing the second step: **Define** the problem. When you formulate a differential and select a treatment plan, you are **Ideating** and **Prototyping** a solution. You run this innovation cycle twenty times a day. We are just changing the 'patient' from a human being to a broken workflow or system."
