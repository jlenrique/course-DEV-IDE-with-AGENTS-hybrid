# Part 3 Knowledge Check: The Opportunity & The Clinician as an Innovator (5 Questions)

**Question 1 (Remember): Multiple Choice**
- **Prompt:** What is the fundamental difference between an "Idea" and an "Opportunity" in the context of medical intrapreneurship?
	- A) An idea has immense intrinsic financial value, while an opportunity is purely conceptual.
	- B) An idea is a vetted plan, while an opportunity is a spontaneous thought.
	- C) An idea has zero intrinsic value on its own, whereas an opportunity is an idea rigorously vetted for feasibility, viability, and desirability with a clear path to execution.
	- D) There is no difference; the terms are used interchangeably in innovation frameworks.
- **Correct Answer:** **C**
- **Explanation:**
	- **Why C is correct:** An idea (like "We should use AI to write notes") has a value of zero on its own. An opportunity is an idea that has been rigorously vetted and engineered to be actionable within a specific organization.
	- **Why A, B, and D are wrong:** These options directly contradict the course's strict definitions and the assertion that ideas start with zero value.

**Question 2 (Remember): Multiple Choice**
- **Prompt:** When mapping the Clinical Method directly to Design Thinking, which Design Thinking step correlates to taking a patient's History?
	- A) Define
	- B) Empathize
	- C) Ideate
	- D) Prototype
- **Correct Answer:** **B**
- **Explanation:**
	- **Why B is correct:** Taking a patient's history requires understanding their pain and experience, which maps perfectly to the "Empathize" step of Design Thinking.
	- **Why A is wrong:** "Define" maps to synthesizing physical exams and labs into a diagnosis.
	- **Why C and D are wrong:** "Ideate" and "Prototype" map to formulating a differential and selecting a treatment plan.

**Question 3 (Understand): Multiple Choice**
- **Prompt:** According to Sandra Lam’s *Intrapreneurship Formula*, what is essential for successfully scaling a solution within an existing hospital?
	- A) Ignoring legacy constraints and operating as a rogue startup.
	- B) Securing external venture capital to bypass the hospital's budget.
	- C) Balancing your clinical vision with the realities of the organizational environment by leveraging existing resources and mastering stakeholder alignment.
	- D) Ensuring the innovation is a standalone product rather than a workflow improvement.
- **Correct Answer:** **C**
- **Explanation:**
	- **Why C is correct:** Lam's formula emphasizes that intrapreneurship requires a calculated approach to balance clinical vision with organizational realities, heavily relying on leveraging existing resources, brand, and internal stakeholder alignment.
	- **Why A, B, and D are wrong:** Intrapreneurship specifically requires working *within* legacy constraints and internal budgets, and it can be a workflow improvement rather than a standalone product.

**Question 4 (Understand): True/False**
- **Statement:** A core conceptual pillar of the course is that medical innovators must "fall in love with their solution" to ensure they maintain the passion required to overcome systemic friction.
- **Correct Answer:** **False**
- **Explanation:**
	- **Why it is False:** The core ethos is the exact opposite: innovators must **"fall in love with the problem."** The text explicitly warns that innovators fail when they become overly attached to their pre-determined solution and ignore the actual systemic root cause.

**Question 5 (Understand): Multiple Choice**
- **Prompt:** Within the context of problem framing, what does identifying the "Lead Domino" accomplish?
	- A) It reveals the most expensive technology available to purchase.
	- B) It identifies the ultimate root cause of a systemic issue, which, when solved, automatically knocks down dozens of downstream symptoms.
	- C) It highlights the clinical staff member responsible for an operational failure so they can be disciplined.
	- D) It provides a superficial workaround to a broken workflow.
- **Correct Answer:** **B**
- **Explanation:**
	- **Why B is correct:** The "Lead Domino" concept asserts that finding and solving the true root cause will automatically resolve the cascading downstream symptoms associated with it.
	- **Why A, C, and D are wrong:** The course focuses on identifying system design failures, not blaming individuals, buying expensive solutions without diagnosis, or creating superficial workarounds.
