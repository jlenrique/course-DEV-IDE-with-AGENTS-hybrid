# **Discussion Post 2: The Worked Example (Ungraded)**

- **Format:** Ungraded, embedded example in Canvas showing a "Good vs. Bad" problem formulation.
- **Context Text:** *"Moving from an 'Idea' to an 'Opportunity' requires rigorous problem framing. Let's look at a worked example of how to elevate your thinking from a superficial complaint to a root-cause analysis. Review the scenario below to prepare for your final graded discussion post at the end of this module."*
- **The Scenario Content:**
	- **The "Bad" Idea (Superficial):** *"My idea is to buy iPads for all the nurses so they can do discharge teaching faster."*
	- **The "Productive Struggle" Breakdown (Audio/Video Narration or Text):** "Why is this a bad start? It jumps straight to a solution (iPads) without defining the actual problem. It assumes technology fixes human workflows. Let's run a root-cause analysis."
	- **The "Good" Reframing (The Opportunity):** *"Chief Complaint: Discharge times are delayed by an average of 3 hours on the med-surg floor. Root Cause: Nurses do not have a standardized, easily accessible checklist for patient education, causing redundant trips to the nursing station. The Opportunity: To design a standardized, mobile-friendly discharge workflow that reduces nursing administrative time and speeds up bed turnover."*
