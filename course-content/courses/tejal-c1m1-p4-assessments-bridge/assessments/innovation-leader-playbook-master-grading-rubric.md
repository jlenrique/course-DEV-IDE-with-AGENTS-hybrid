# Innovation Leader Playbook: Master Grading Rubric

## Grading Scale

- **Exemplary (4 points):** Demonstrates mastery, deep synthesis of concepts, and executive-level readiness.
- **Proficient (3 points):** Demonstrates strong understanding and application, with minor gaps in depth or framing.
- **Developing (2 points):** Shows basic comprehension but relies on superficial analysis or incomplete application of frameworks.
- **Needs Improvement (1 point):** Fails to apply course concepts; responses are incomplete, defensive, or lack clinical/organizational relevance.

## 1. Analytical Depth and Problem Framing

Evaluates the learner's ability to move from a surface-level chief complaint to a systemic root cause.

- **Exemplary:** Relentlessly interrogates the problem. Successfully isolates the systemic or structural root cause using the 5 Whys or similar logic instead of blaming individuals or stopping at superficial symptoms. Problem statements are highly specific.
- **Proficient:** Identifies a valid problem and attempts root-cause analysis, but the final problem statement may be too broad or focused on a secondary symptom.
- **Developing:** Problem statements read as standard workplace complaints. There is little evidence of digging beneath the surface. The learner jumps to solutions before defining the problem.
- **Needs Improvement:** Problem statements are vague, emotional, or misdiagnose the systemic issue.

## 2. Application of Innovation Frameworks

Evaluates how accurately the learner integrates module-specific tools such as Design Thinking, the Intrapreneurship Formula, and the Opportunity Radar.

- **Exemplary:** Seamlessly and accurately maps clinical experiences to prescribed innovation frameworks. Uses course vocabulary such as Intelligent Failure, Psychological Safety, and Opportunity vs. Idea correctly and in context.
- **Proficient:** Accurately applies frameworks, though the connection between the clinical scenario and the theoretical framework may occasionally feel forced.
- **Developing:** Mentions frameworks in passing but does not actively use them to shape analysis. Misapplies core concepts.
- **Needs Improvement:** Ignores the prescribed frameworks and relies on personal opinion or outdated management assumptions.

## 3. Organizational Feasibility and Actionability

Evaluates the learner's situational awareness as an intrapreneur navigating a complex health system.

- **Exemplary:** Demonstrates acute awareness of sphere of influence and constraints such as budget, IT limitations, and political dynamics. Proposed opportunities are actionable and realistic.
- **Proficient:** Proposes actionable opportunities but may underestimate organizational resistance or required resources.
- **Developing:** Ideas are theoretical or require unrealistic resources. The learner lacks intrapreneurial constraint awareness.
- **Needs Improvement:** Ideas are disconnected from healthcare operations or outside the learner's sphere of influence.

## 4. Reflective Vulnerability and Mindset

Evaluates the learner's capacity for self-critique, growth mindset, and psychological safety.

- **Exemplary:** Exhibits high reflective vulnerability. Identifies personal blind spots, fixed-mindset triggers, or departmental failures without defensiveness. Treats past failures as data.
- **Proficient:** Shows willingness to reflect and adapt, though may hedge when discussing weaknesses or failures.
- **Developing:** Reflection is guarded or superficial. Shows signs of a fixed mindset by blaming external factors exclusively.
- **Needs Improvement:** Refuses to acknowledge blind spots or systemic flaws.

## 5. Executive Communication

Evaluates clarity, concision, and professionalism.

- **Exemplary:** Writing is crisp, concise, and punchy. Uses formatting to make the entry scannable for an executive stakeholder.
- **Proficient:** Clear and professional, with occasional unnecessary jargon or density.
- **Developing:** Disorganized or rambling. The reader must work to find the core argument.
- **Needs Improvement:** Unprofessional, grammatically weak, or incoherent.

## Faculty Guide

- **18-20 points:** Honors / Pass. The entry is portfolio-ready.
- **14-17 points:** Pass. The entry is solid but requires minor refinement.
- **Under 14 points:** Revise and resubmit. The learner is still thinking as a clinical reactor rather than an innovator.
