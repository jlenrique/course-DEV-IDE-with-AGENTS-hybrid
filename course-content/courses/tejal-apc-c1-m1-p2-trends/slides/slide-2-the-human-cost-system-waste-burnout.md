# **Slide 2: The Human Cost: System Waste & Burnout**

- **Visual Format:** Infographic with two stark, contrasting statistics.
- **Narration (Speaker Notes):** "These massive, complex systems are currently incredibly inefficient. Roughly 25% of total U.S. healthcare spending goes toward administrative costs and waste, not patient care. But the true cost of this inefficiency is human. Over half of physicians report symptoms of burnout. Crucially, the top driver of this burnout is not patient volume; it is bureaucratic tasks and administrative bloat. This is a profound insight. The burnout epidemic is not a personal failing of resilience; it is a system design problem. The workarounds we invent daily to survive a poorly designed EHR or a broken admission process are the exact friction points that are ripe for innovation."

**References:** Shrank WH et al., JAMA (2019); Medscape Physician Burnout Report (2024).
