	## Introduction {toggle="true"}
		### **Learning Objectives** {toggle="true"}
			**This module directly supports the following Course 1 Goal:**
			To equip learners with the foundational mindsets and skills to identify, validate, and articulate a compelling opportunity for innovation within their clinical environment.*** ***
			***By the end of this module, the learner will be able to:***
			- ***MO 1.1:** Definition of the innovation mindset, why it is important and common misconceptions.*
			- ***MO 1.2:** Analyze the impact of psychological safety and fixed/growth mindsets on clinical team performance, patient safety, and innovation.*
			- ***MO 1.3:** Apply the "Intelligent Failure" framework to distinguish between blameworthy and praiseworthy failures in a clinical setting.*
			- ***MO 1.4:** Evaluate one's personal innovation mindset, goals, and professional environment through a structured SWOT analysis.*

	## **Part 1: The Call - Setting the Stage** {toggle="true"}
		### Page 3:** Case for Change** {toggle="true"}
			**Chapter 3: Case for Change**
			- Introduction: Healthcare is undergoing structural transformation. Physician roles, practice models, and organizational expectations are shifting rapidly, yet professional preparation has not evolved at the same pace. While there is substantial interest among physicians in leadership and systems-level influence, formal pathways, training, and institutional infrastructure remain limited. At the same time, consolidation of practice settings and increasing employment within large health systems are reshaping autonomy, decision-making authority, and accountability structures. This chapter examines the data behind these shifts and establishes why healthcare is not simply changing, but positioned at an inflection point that requires new models of leadership and innovation.<br>
