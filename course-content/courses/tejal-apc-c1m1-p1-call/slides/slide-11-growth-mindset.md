# **Slide 11: Expectations — The Growth Mindset (Standalone Block 1 of 2)**

- **Visual Format:** Column visual with imagery describing growth mindset, carrying its formal definition. (Part of the standalone Expectations & Mental Frameworks mini-presentation, split out in post-production.)
- **Key Text / Definition:** **Growth Mindset** — the belief that clinical and leadership capabilities are not fixed traits, but can be developed through dedication and iteration.
- **Narration (Speaker Notes):** "To navigate this lineage, you must cultivate two foundational elements: a Growth Mindset and Psychological Safety. A growth mindset, coined by Carol Dweck, is the belief that your capacity to innovate is not fixed; it is a muscle built through effort and critique."
