# **Slide 7: The Innovation Lexicon (Definition Box)**

- **Visual Format:** A clean, high-contrast "Reference Box" slide that learners can screenshot or download.
- **Key Text / Definitions:**
    - **Innovation:** Creating new value that is adopted by the market/system. (It is *not* just inventing a new gadget).
    - **Intrapreneurship:** Acting like an entrepreneur, but driving the innovation from *within* the walls of an existing, established organization (e.g., a hospital system) while navigating its legacy constraints.
    - **Systemic Friction:** The chronic, recurring operational or clinical pain points that drain time, money, and morale from a system.
    - **Root Cause vs. Symptom:** A symptom is the visible pain (e.g., high nursing turnover). The root cause is the invisible system failure creating the pain (e.g., a broken EHR triage workflow).
    - **Functional Flexibility:** The ability to adapt your skills and borrow concepts from outside your clinical specialty to solve ambiguous problems.
    - **First Principles Thinking:** Breaking down a complex, systemic problem into its most basic, foundational truths, and building a solution up from there, rather than reasoning by analogy or "how we have always done it."
    - **Psychological Safety:** The shared belief that a team environment is safe for interpersonal risk-taking; the ability to report errors, ask questions, or propose unconventional ideas without fear of humiliation or professional retaliation.
    - **Growth Mindset:** The belief that clinical and leadership capabilities are not fixed traits, but can be developed through dedication and iteration. It shifts the response to failure from "I am not equipped for this" to "What data did this failure give us to improve the system?"
- **Narration (Speaker Notes):** "Before we dive into problem framing, we must establish a shared language. In this program, words have very specific meanings. 'Innovation' is not just a buzzword; it is the creation of adopted value. We are not teaching you to be rogue entrepreneurs; we are teaching you to be 'Intrapreneurs'—leading change from within your own hospitals. Furthermore, the bottom three definitions represent the psychological engine of this course. You cannot innovate using 'First Principles' if your team lacks 'Psychological Safety,' and you will not survive the inevitable setbacks without a 'Growth Mindset.' Take a moment to review this lexicon, as these definitions will serve as the guardrails for the rest of the program."
