# Chapter 1 Knowledge Check: Setting the Stage (5 Questions)

Parsed as a lesson component for downstream use per operator directive (not produced in the deck; feeds workbook course-check instruments).

**Question 1 (Remember): Multiple Choice** Which of the following best defines "intrapreneurship" according to the course's innovation lexicon? A) Leaving a hospital system to start a venture-backed healthcare technology company. B) Acting like an entrepreneur by driving innovation from *within* the walls of an existing, established organization. C) Purchasing and implementing a new software tool created by a third-party vendor. D) The clinical act of diagnosing a rare disease using first principles thinking.

- **Correct Answer:** B — Intrapreneurship specifically refers to utilizing entrepreneurial principles to drive change and implement innovative solutions from inside an existing organization while navigating its legacy constraints.

**Question 2 (Understand): Multiple Choice** Which of the following best describes the use of "First Principles Thinking" when approaching systemic healthcare problems? A) Implementing solutions based on analogies and relying on how the hospital has historically operated. B) Breaking down a complex, systemic problem into its most basic, foundational truths and building a new solution up from there. C) Immediately purchasing the most popular software tool used by competitor health systems. D) Establishing a committee to assign individual blame for systemic errors during M&M conferences.

- **Correct Answer:** B — First Principles Thinking breaks a complex systemic problem into foundational truths rather than reasoning by analogy or precedent.

**Question 3 (Understand): Multiple Choice** How does the curriculum define adopting an "innovation mindset" for the modern clinician? A) Shifting focus entirely away from clinical duties to administrative management. B) The internal conviction that a clinician is not just an employee within the healthcare system, but an empowered designer of it. C) A commitment to adopting every new piece of technology released in the medical field. D) The belief that systemic healthcare problems can only be solved by hospital executives.

- **Correct Answer:** B — the innovation mindset is the conviction that clinicians are empowered designers of the system, bridging current limitations and future possibilities.

**Question 4 (Understand): Multiple Choice** According to David Epstein's concepts discussed, why is "functional flexibility" crucial for a physician-innovator? A) Because clinical medicine is a "wicked" environment with clear, repetitive rules. B) Because physicians need physical flexibility to navigate long hospital shifts. C) Because healthcare innovation is a "wicked" environment, requiring the humility to borrow broad, interdisciplinary concepts to solve ambiguous problems. D) Because hyper-specialization is the only way to solve systemic operational friction.

- **Correct Answer:** C — systemic problems are "wicked" environments; generalists with Range succeed by borrowing concepts across domains.

**Question 5 (Understand): True/False** Statement: When evaluating systemic friction, high nursing turnover is considered a root cause, whereas a broken electronic health record (EHR) triage workflow is a symptom.

- **Correct Answer:** False — the relationship is reversed: a symptom is the visible pain (high turnover); the root cause is the invisible system failure creating it (broken EHR workflow).
