		**Question 1 (Remember): Multiple Choice** What is the fundamental difference between an "Idea" and an "Opportunity" in the context of medical intrapreneurship? A) An idea has immense intrinsic financial value, while an opportunity is purely conceptual. B) An idea is a vetted plan, while an opportunity is a spontaneous thought. C) An idea has zero intrinsic value on its own, whereas an opportunity is an idea rigorously vetted for feasibility, viability, and desirability with a clear path to execution. D) There is no difference; the terms are used interchangeably in innovation frameworks.
		- **Correct Answer:** **C**
		- **Explanation:**
			- **Why C is correct:** An idea (like "We should use AI to write notes") has a value of zero on its own. An opportunity is an idea that has been rigorously vetted and engineered to be actionable within a specific organization.
			- **Why A, B, and D are wrong:** These options directly contradict the course's strict definitions and the assertion that ideas start with zero value.
		**Question 2 (Remember): Multiple Choice** When mapping the Clinical Method directly to Design Thinking, which Design Thinking step correlates to taking a patient's History? A) Define B) Empathize C) Ideate D) Prototype
		- **Correct Answer:** **B**
		- **Explanation:**
			- **Why B is correct:** Taking a patient's history requires understanding their pain and experience, which maps perfectly to the "Empathize" step of Design Thinking.
			- **Why A is wrong:** "Define" maps to synthesizing physical exams and labs into a diagnosis.
			- **Why C and D are wrong:** "Ideate" and "Prototype" map to formulating a differential and selecting a treatment plan.
		**Question 3 (Understand): Multiple Choice** According to Sandra Lam’s *Intrapreneurship Formula*, what is essential for successfully scaling a solution within an existing hospital? C) Balancing your clinical vision with the realities of the organizational environment by leveraging existing resources and mastering stakeholder alignment. D) Ensuring the innovation is a standalone product rather than a workflow improvement.
		- **Correct Answer:** **C**
		- **Explanation:**
			- **Why C is correct:** Lam's formula emphasizes that intrapreneurship requires a calculated approach to balance clinical vision with organizational realities, heavily relying on leveraging existing resources, brand, and internal stakeholder alignment.
			- **Why A, B, and D are wrong:** Intrapreneurship specifically requires working *within* legacy constraints and internal budgets, and it can be a workflow improvement rather than a standalone product.
