## Slide 2 — Data / Figure: Healthcare Waste

Healthcare Waste

The estimated cost of waste in the US health care system ranged from $760 billion to $935 billion, accounting for approximately 25% of total health care spending, and the projected potential savings from interventions that reduce waste, excluding savings from administrative complexity, ranged from $191 billion to $286 billion, representing a potential 25% reduction in the total cost of waste. (Shrank WH, Rogstad TL, Parekh N. Waste in the US Health Care System: Estimated Costs and Potential for Savings. JAMA. 2019;322(15):1501–1509.)

Approximately 25% of total U.S. healthcare spending (nearly $1 trillion) goes toward administrative costs — not patient care. The U.S. healthcare system currently ranks last among high-income nations in efficiency and equity, despite having the highest spending. (McKinsey & Company / The Commonwealth Fund "Mirror, Mirror" Report, 2024)

## Slide 3 — Human Vignette: The Modern Clinician's Dilemma

The Modern Clinician's Dilemma

The landscape of medicine is changing at an unprecedented pace. You are practicing in an era of immense technological advancement, shifting payment models, and increasing system complexity. However, traditional medical education, while excellent at creating clinical experts, often falls short in preparing future clinical leaders to navigate and shape this new environment. You are taught to follow evidence-based pathways, but not how to create the pathways of the future.

This creates a critical gap — a system full of brilliant problem-solvers who feel disempowered to fix the very systems they work within. The primary barrier is often not a lack of ideas, but the absence of a structured process and the psychological safety to pursue them.

We believe the solution to healthcare's biggest challenges lies dormant within its most valuable asset: you. Your proximity to the patient, your deep domain expertise, and your intrinsic drive to heal make you the ideal agent of change.

## Slide 4 — Concept / Framework: Expectations & Mental Frameworks

Expectations & Mental Frameworks

To succeed in this journey, we will help you build and refine a set of powerful mental frameworks that are as critical to innovation as they are to clinical excellence:

- Resiliency & Agility: Learning to adapt to setbacks and pivot when an approach isn't working.
- First Principles Thinking: Breaking down complex problems to their most fundamental truths to unlock novel solutions, rather than relying on analogy or convention.
- Embracing Intelligent Failure: Shifting the view of failure from something to be avoided at all costs to a necessary and valuable source of data in the innovation process.
- Growth Mindset: The belief that clinical and leadership capabilities are not fixed traits, but can be developed through dedication and iteration.
